## Competitor-by-competitor

### 🟧 Bow's Journeys — *the most direct rival*
**Site:** bowsjourneys.com · **Advertiser:** Boudewijn Allard Metzelaar (verified)

**Live ad copy:**
- "Private Canal Cruises — Enjoy a private Amsterdam canal cruise. Luxury boats, your pace."
- "Beautiful historic salonboat, indoor & outdoor seating, all inclusive."
- "20+ Years Experience — Amsterdam's canals on a fully electric boat."
- "Private and shared tours with local captains. Experience canals differently."
- "All-inclusive small group boutique canal cruise, max 12 guests."

**Why they're the biggest threat:** they hug Off Course's exact product — private + shared, fully electric, local captains, small-group, max 12, "experience canals differently." You will be in the **same auctions** as Bow's on "private canal cruise amsterdam."

**Their tilt:** established, tasteful, upscale — "20+ years," "historic salonboat," "in style," "all-inclusive."

**How Off Course wins:** you can't out-luxury or out-experience a 20-year operator, so don't. Win on the **opposite register** — warm, modern, effortless, "your friend with a boat," zero pretension. Same boat specs, opposite emotional vibe. And use **Quality Score** (tight keyword→ad→landing-page relevance) to pay less than Bow's for the same position.

### 🟨 Boatnow — *the marketplace / price play*
**Site:** boatnow.com · **80 ads**, multilingual (EN / NL / DE), full extensions

**Live ad copy:**
- "Rent a Private Boat — 117 Luxury Boats in Amsterdam. Lowest price guaranteed. Captain included. Over 117 boats & 114 pickup-points."
- (NL) "Volledig verzorgd privé rondvaarten op maat voor 2-80 personen. Vanaf €140 p.u."

**Their tilt:** faceless aggregator — scale, "lowest price guaranteed," 117 boats. Attracts bargain-hunters, not experience-seekers.

**How Off Course wins:** never fight on price or boat-count. Avoid their generic "boat rental amsterdam" head terms (expensive, they dominate). They have no soul — one real boat + one real skipper beats a catalogue of 117 for the customer who wants an *experience*.

### 🟧 Those Dam Boat Guys — *the fun / irreverent brand*
**Site:** thosedamboatguys.com · **~78 ads**, verified

**Their tilt:** irreverent, young, electric, no-script — the closest *vibe* to Off Course, but skews backpacker/party and **public hop-on**, not private.

**How Off Course wins:** out-*intimate* them, don't out-irreverent them. Private (your own boat) vs their shared hop-on; warm local-host vibe (your Tariq persona) vs their rowdier energy.

## What this means for the campaign

1. **Lean HARD into the warm/unpretentious voice.** It's the only thing that separates you from Bow's and Private Canal Cruises, who match your product spec-for-spec. Make sure a couple of headlines really hammer "friend with a boat / real Amsterdam / no pretension."
2. **Don't claim luxury or "in style."** You can't beat 20-year saloon-boat operators at their own game, and it's off-brand. Owning the opposite is your moat.
3. **Quality Score is your cost weapon vs Bow's & Private Canal Cruises.** You'll share their auctions; tighter relevance = cheaper clicks for the same position. Your private-only keyword set + matched copy + converting listing page is built for this.
4. **A lower headline price is an edge.** Competitors push "all-inclusive / luxury." Your "Private Boat from €165" looks cheaper at the click — while you still make the drinks margin on board.
5. **"Occasions" is proven demand.** Pure Boats and Private Canal Cruises both run proposal / romantic / dinner ads. A future ad group around birthdays, anniversaries, proposals, "friends visiting" is validated by the market.
6. **Extensions are table stakes** — every serious competitor runs them. Off Course's new sitelinks / callouts / structured snippets bring you to parity.
